**(0:30 – 1:30) — CONTEXT**

Jo main aapko bataane wala hoon — yeh sirf meri kahani nahi hai. Main ne Sweden mein dojanon Pakistani immigrants se baat ki hai aur pehle 90 din ke experiences seriously milte julte nikle.

[What I'm about to tell you — this isn't just my story. I've talked to dozens of Pakistani immigrants in Sweden and the first 90-day experiences turned out to be seriously similar.]

Aur ek cheez sab mein ek jesi thi: jo woh expect kar ke aaye the woh nahi mila. Lekin jo mila — usne unhe mazboot banaya.

[And one thing was the same in all: what they expected when they came, they didn't find. But what they found — made them stronger.]

Yeh video un logon ke liye hai jo Sweden aana chahte hain — ya abhi pohonche hain — aur sochte hain ke woh akele hain is confusion mein. Aap nahi hain.

[This video is for those who want to come to Sweden — or have just arrived — and think they're alone in this confusion. You're not.]

**(1:30 – 4:00) — DIN 1 — AIRPORT SE AAGE KYA HOTA HAI**

Main jab Stockholm Arlanda Airport par utara — pehli feeling excitement thi. Naya mulk. Naya baab.

[When I landed at Stockholm Arlanda Airport — the first feeling was excitement. New country. New chapter.]

Phir airport se bahar nikla. February tha. Andheera tha. Teen baj ke the dopahar mein — aur andheera tha.

[Then I came out of the airport. It was February. It was dark. It was 3 in the afternoon — and it was dark.]

Sweden mein sardion mein 4 baje tak raat ho jaati hai. Yeh mujhe kisi ne nahi bataya tha. Aur iska psychological asar hota hai — seriously, believe karo ya na karo.

[In Sweden in winter it gets dark by 4pm. Nobody had told me this. And it has a psychological effect — seriously, believe it or not.]

Pehle din ka practical checklist:

SIM card — airport par Telia aur Tre ki kiosks hoti hain. Unlimited data monthly plan SEK 200 se 300 mein. Yeh pehla kaam hai.

[SIM card — there are Telia and Tre kiosks at the airport. Unlimited data monthly plan in SEK 200 to 300. This is the first task.]

Accommodation confirm karo — chahe koi bhi arrangement ho. Mera accommodation woh nahi nikla jo WhatsApp par confirm hua tha — lockbox code galat tha. Raat 9 baje, minus 8 degree mein, bahar khada tha main. Lesson: ek backup number rakh lo.

[Confirm accommodation — whatever arrangement it is. My accommodation wasn't what was confirmed on WhatsApp — the lockbox code was wrong. At 9pm, in minus 8 degrees, I was standing outside. Lesson: keep one backup number.]

Nearest ICA ya Coop — Sweden ki sabse common grocery chains. Google Maps se pehle din hi dhundh lo.

[Nearest ICA or Coop — Sweden's most common grocery chains. Find it on Google Maps on the very first day.]

Pakistani community Facebook group — "Pakistani in Stockholm" — same day join karo. Emergencies, accommodation tips, kaam ke leads — yeh network pehle hafte mein kisi official system se honestly zyada kaam aata hai.

[Pakistani community Facebook group — "Pakistani in Stockholm" — join the same day. Emergencies, accommodation tips, work leads — this network honestly works more in the first week than any official system.]

**(4:00 – 6:30) — HAFTE 1 — PERSONNUMMER KE BINA ZINDAGI**

Personnummer — yeh Swedish personal identity number hai. Format: YYYYMMDD-XXXX. Aur Sweden mein bina personnummer ke — literally kuch nahi hota.

[Personnummer — this is the Swedish personal identity number. Format: YYYYMMDD-XXXX. And in Sweden without personnummer — literally nothing works.]

Bank account nahi khulta. SFI mein register nahi ho saktay. Healthcare properly access nahi hoti. Zyada tar apartments nahi mil sakte.

[Bank account doesn't open. Can't register for SFI. Healthcare can't be properly accessed. Most apartments can't be found.]

Personnummer kahan se milta hai — Skatteverket, yaani Swedish Tax Agency. Yahan register karna hoga. Work permit holders ke liye online ya walk-in appointment leni hogi. Stockholm mein walk-in possible hai.

[Where to get personnummer — Skatteverket, meaning Swedish Tax Agency. You'll need to register here. For work permit holders you'll need an online or walk-in appointment. Walk-in is possible in Stockholm.]

Documents chahiye: valid passport, work permit, proof of accommodation — lease ya host ki taraf se letter — aur employment contract.

[Documents needed: valid passport, work permit, proof of accommodation — lease or letter from host — and employment contract.]

Timeline: appointment milne mein kuch hafte. Personnummer aane mein uske baad aur waqt. Directly Skatteverket.se par check karo — current timelines wahan milenge.

[Timeline: a few weeks to get appointment. More time for personnummer to arrive after that. Check directly on Skatteverket.se — current timelines will be there.]

Matlab pehle kaafi hafte bina personnummer ke. Yeh Sweden ki ek seriously painful reality hai.

[Meaning the first several weeks without personnummer. This is one seriously painful reality of Sweden.]

Is period mein bank account ke liye Wise ya Revolut use karo. Bina personnummer ke kaam karte hain. Swedish employer yeh accept kar leta hai beginning mein — lekin pehle se employer se baat kar lo.

[During this period use Wise or Revolut for bank account. They work without personnummer. Swedish employer accepts this at the beginning — but talk to the employer in advance.]

**(6:00 – 8:30) — EMOTIONAL TIMELINE — JO KOI NAHI BATAATA**

Pehle do hafte: excitement aur energy.
Sab kuch naya hai. Explore kar rahay ho. Har cheez interesting lagti hai — Swedish traffic lights ki awaaz bhi. Energy bula hai.

[First two weeks: excitement and energy.
Everything is new. You're exploring. Everything seems interesting — even the sound of Swedish traffic lights. Energy is high.]

Teesre aur chauthe hafte: practical frustration.
Personnummer nahi aaya. Bank account nahi khula. Ghar ki situation uncertain hai. Kaam shuru ho gayi hai — lekin sab kuch adjust ho raha hai. Thakawat shuru hoti hai.

[Third and fourth week: practical frustration.
Personnummer hasn't come. Bank account hasn't opened. Home situation is uncertain. Work has started — but everything is still adjusting. Fatigue starts.]

Paanchve se aathve hafte tak: tanhaayi.
Yeh woh phase hai jo Pakistan se aane wale taqreeban sab ke liye aata hai — aur jis ke baray mein koi baat nahi karta.

[Fifth to eighth week: loneliness.
This is the phase that comes for almost everyone coming from Pakistan — and that nobody talks about.]

Sweden mein social culture ek specific tareeqe se kaam karta hai. Swedes apne existing dost circles mein comfortable hote hain. Naye log — khaas taur par immigrants — ke liye organic dostiyaan bahut aahista develop hoti hain.

[Social culture in Sweden works in a specific way. Swedes are comfortable in their existing friend circles. For new people — especially immigrants — organic friendships develop very slowly.]

Aap office mein kaam kar rahay ho, lunch mein koi invite nahi karta — kyunke yeh honestly Sweden ka tareeqa nahi hai. Swedes aksar apni desk par ya ghar mein lunch karte hain. Aapko khud initiative lena hoga.

[You're working in office, nobody invites you to lunch — because honestly this isn't Sweden's way. Swedes often have lunch at their own desk or at home. You'll need to take the initiative yourself.]

---

Ek raat main ne Stockholm mein walk ki — minus 12 degree mein — kyunke apartment mein akela baithna unbearable ho gayi thi. Main ne ammi ko call kiya Pakistan. Woh rone lagin. Main roya. Phir hum dono hanse — kyunke hum dono jaante the ke yeh phase hai. Phase hamesha nahi rehta.

[One night I walked in Stockholm — in minus 12 degrees — because sitting alone in the apartment had become unbearable. I called mom in Pakistan. She started crying. I cried. Then we both laughed — because we both knew this was a phase. A phase doesn't last forever.]

---

Nauve se barhve hafte tak: routine aur anchor dhundhna.
Jo log Sweden mein adjust ho jaate hain — woh routine banana shuru karte hain. Ek cheez jo mujhe personally sabse zyada help ki: Fajr ke baad 20 minute ki walk. Ek pakka anchor. Roz subah aisi cheez jo meri apni thi — Sweden ki nahi, Pakistan ki nahi — bas meri.

[Ninth to twelfth week: finding routine and anchor.
Those who adjust in Sweden — they start building routines. One thing that personally helped me the most: a 20-minute walk after Fajr. A firm anchor. Every morning something that was mine — not Sweden's, not Pakistan's — just mine.]

Yeh 90-day checklist hai:

[This is the 90-day checklist:]

Din 1 se 7:
Swedish SIM card. Temporary accommodation confirmed. Skatteverket appointment booked — skatteverket.se par. Pakistani community Facebook group. ICA ya Coop location. Wise ya Revolut account tayar.

[Day 1 to 7:
Swedish SIM card. Temporary accommodation confirmed. Skatteverket appointment booked — on skatteverket.se. Pakistani community Facebook group. ICA or Coop location. Wise or Revolut account ready.]

Din 8 se 30:
Personnummer received. Swedish bank account — Handelsbanken, SEB, Swedbank. SFI registration at local municipality — personnummer aate hi. Healthcare registration: nearest Vårdcentral mein register. Public transport card — Stockholm mein SL card.

[Day 8 to 30:
Personnummer received. Swedish bank account — Handelsbanken, SEB, Swedbank. SFI registration at local municipality — as soon as personnummer comes. Healthcare registration: register at nearest Vårdcentral. Public transport card — SL card in Stockholm.]

Din 31 se 60:
Pehli SFI class. Kam az kam ek Swedish colleague se mukhtasar baat. Koi ek chota kaam Swedish mein akele karo — pharmacy, grocery, post office. Housing queue register at Bostadsförmedlingen.se. Pehli tankhwah aane par tax code verify karo.

[Day 31 to 60:
First SFI class. At least one brief conversation with a Swedish colleague. One small errand in Swedish alone — pharmacy, grocery, post office. Housing queue register at Bostadsförmedlingen.se. When first salary comes verify tax code.]

Din 61 se 90:
Swedish routine qaim. Kam az kam ek local connection — masjid, sport club, colleague. Roz basic Swedish phrases use hone lagin. 90-din reflection: kya kaam aaya, agle 90 dinon mein kya theek karna hai.

[Day 61 to 90:
Swedish routine established. At least one local connection — mosque, sport club, colleague. Basic Swedish phrases starting to be used daily. 90-day reflection: what worked, what to fix in next 90 days.]

**(10:30 – 12:30) — SWEDEN JO BROCHURES MEIN NAHI HAI**

Pehli baat: Swedes bohot achhe hain — lekin woh chahte hain ke aap pehle qadam barhain.
Sweden ka reputation hai thanda aur door ka. Yeh aadha sach hai. Swedes khud se aapki taraf nahi aate — lekin agar aap genuinely unki taraf jaate hain, woh warmly respond karte hain. Pehla qadam aap karein.

[First thing: Swedes are very good — but they want you to take the first step.
Sweden has a reputation for being cold and distant. That's half true. Swedes don't come towards you on their own — but if you go to them genuinely, they respond warmly. You take the first step.]

Doosri baat: Sweden mein aapke iman ki izzat ki jaati hai — lekin yeh zaati baat hai.
Sweden secular hai. Log kaam ki jagah par aam taur par apna mazhab khule aam nahi batate. Yeh na anti-religion hai, na rejection — yeh sirf Swedish culture hai. Prayer breaks, halal khana, Ramadan — in sab ke baray mein employers se baat kar saktay hain. Zyada tar Swedish employers professionally accommodate karte hain.

[Second thing: your faith is respected in Sweden — but it's personal.
Sweden is secular. People generally don't openly tell their religion at work. This is neither anti-religion, nor rejection — this is just Swedish culture. Prayer breaks, halal food, Ramadan — you can talk to employers about all of these. Most Swedish employers accommodate professionally.]

Teesri baat: Swedish fitrat — yeh seriously therapy hai.
Pakistan se ana — khaas taur par shahron se — toh Sweden ki khamoshi ajanbi lagti hai. Lekin jo log outdoor culture adopt karte hain — hiking, cycling, frozen lake par chalna — woh Sweden mein kahin zyada khush rahte hain. Aur yeh sab bilkul muft hai.

[Third thing: Swedish nature — it's seriously therapy.
Coming from Pakistan — especially from cities — Sweden's silence seems foreign. But those who adopt outdoor culture — hiking, cycling, walking on frozen lakes — they are much happier in Sweden. And all of this is completely free.]

Chauthi baat: punctuality — yeh serious hai.
10 minute late = unprofessional. Meeting 9 baje hai — aap 8:57 par wahan ho. Yeh Pakistani culture se ek zaroori adjustment hai. Pehle 90 din har jagah 10 minute early pohoncho.

[Fourth thing: punctuality — this is serious.
10 minutes late = unprofessional. Meeting is at 9 — you're there at 8:57. This is a necessary adjustment from Pakistani culture. For the first 90 days arrive 10 minutes early everywhere.]

Paanchvi baat: Swedes seedha feedback dena aur lena pasand karte hain.
Agar kisi ko aapke saath koi masla hai — woh seedha aapko batayenge. Nahi muskuraenge, nahi peeth piche baat karenge. Pehle yeh rude lagta hai — phir ahsaas hota hai ke yeh actually respectful hai.

[Fifth thing: Swedes like giving and receiving direct feedback.
If someone has a problem with you — they'll tell you directly. They won't smile, won't talk behind your back. Initially this seems rude — then you realize that this is actually respectful.]
