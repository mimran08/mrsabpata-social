**(0:00 – 0:30) — HOOK**

Main ne Sweden mein Pakistani candidates ko baar baar ek hi wajah se fail hote dekha hai. Aur woh wajah skills nahi thi.

[I've seen Pakistani candidates fail in Sweden again and again for the same reason. And that reason wasn't skills.]

[They were prepared for a Pakistani interview. Not a Swedish one.]

Aaj exactly bataata hoon ke Swedish HR kya expect karta hai — aur woh galtiyan jo aapki application seedha reject karwa deti hain.

[Today I'll tell you exactly what Swedish HR expects — and those mistakes that get your application rejected outright.]

---

**(0:30 – 1:30) — CONTEXT**

Sweden ke job interviews basically fundamentally alag hain South Asian interviews se. Yeh farq sirf language ka nahi — yeh farq structure ka hai, expectations ka hai, aur un unspoken rules ka hai jo koi nahi bataata.

[Sweden's job interviews are basically fundamentally different from South Asian interviews. This difference isn't just language — this difference is in structure, in expectations, and in those unspoken rules nobody tells you.]

Jo cheez Pakistan mein adab dikhana tha — boss ko "sir" kehna, apni success ka credit team ko dena, aajzi dikhana — woh Sweden mein honestly weakness signal karta hai.

[What showed respect in Pakistan — calling the boss "sir", giving credit to the team for your success, showing humility — honestly signals weakness in Sweden.]

Aur jo cheez Sweden mein strength hai — seedha feedback dena, apni raaye boldly kehna, kisi se khule aam ikhtilaf karna — woh Pakistan mein takabbur lagti hai.

[And what is strength in Sweden — giving direct feedback, boldly stating your opinion, openly disagreeing with someone — seems arrogant in Pakistan.]

Aaj woh mental shift deta hoon jo Swedish interview pass karne ke liye seriously zaroori hai.

[Today I'll give you that mental shift that is seriously necessary to pass a Swedish interview.]

---

**(1:30 – 3:30) — SWEDISH INTERVIEW KA STRUCTURE**

Sweden mein zyada tar companies competency-based interviews karte hain. Basically matlab:

[In Sweden most companies conduct competency-based interviews. Basically meaning:]

"Tell me about a time when..." — yeh Sweden ka favourite interview format hai.

["Tell me about a time when..." — this is Sweden's favourite interview format.]

Woh yeh nahi poochenge: "Aap kitni mehnat karte hain?" Ya "Apni ek kamzori bataao."

[They won't ask: "How hard do you work?" Or "Tell me one weakness."]

Woh yeh poochenge:
"Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback to a colleague."
"Tell me about a project that failed. What was your role and what did you learn?"
"Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. What did you do?"
"Tell me about a time you had to work without clear instructions."

[They'll ask:
"Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback to a colleague."
"Tell me about a project that failed. What was your role and what did you learn?"
"Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. What did you do?"
"Tell me about a time you had to work without clear instructions."]

Yeh sawal specifically test karte hain: initiative, ikhtilaf bardasht karna, aur apni galtiyon ki zimmedari lena.

[These questions specifically test: initiative, tolerance for disagreement, and taking responsibility for your own mistakes.]

Pakistan mein hum galti ka credit team ko dete hain — "hum sab ne milke galti ki." Sweden mein honestly yeh galat jawab hai. Sweden mein woh sunna chahte hain: "MAIN ne yeh faisla kiya, MAIN ne yeh galti ki, aur main ne isse is tarah theek kiya."

[In Pakistan we give the team credit for mistakes — "we all made this mistake together." In Sweden honestly this is the wrong answer. In Sweden they want to hear: "I made this decision, I made this mistake, and I fixed it this way."]

Mujhe personally ek Swedish HR manager se milne ka mauqa mila. Unhone kaha: "Jab bhi koi candidate 'hum' kehta hai — main fauran sochti hoon: kya yeh banda zimmedari leta hai? Jo candidates 'main' clearly kehte hain — chahe galti ke liye bhi — woh alag nazar aate hain."

[I personally got the chance to meet a Swedish HR manager. She said: "Whenever a candidate says 'we' — I immediately think: does this person take responsibility? Candidates who clearly say 'I' — even for mistakes — they stand out."]

---

**(3:30 – 5:30) — PAKISTANI HABITS JO SWEDEN MEIN BACKFIRE HOTI HAIN**

Yeh galtiyan isliye bata raha hoon kyunke main ne doosron ko karte dekha hai — aur honestly khud bhi kuch ki hain.

[I'm telling these mistakes because I've seen others make them — and honestly I've made some myself too.]

Pehli galti — zyada aajzi.
Pakistan mein aajzi achi cheez hai. Swedish interview mein — agar aap apni kamiyabian clearly nahi bol saktay — recruiter assume karta hai ke woh kamiyabian hain hi nahi. "Team ka kaam tha" — Sweden mein yeh shukriya nahi, yeh red flag hai.

[First mistake — too much humility.
Humility is a good thing in Pakistan. In a Swedish interview — if you can't clearly state your achievements — the recruiter assumes those achievements don't exist. "It was the team's work" — in Sweden this isn't thanks, it's a red flag.]

Doosri galti — "sir" aur "madam" kehna.
Sweden mein first name culture hai — boss ko bhi first name se bulao. Agar aap "yes sir" keh dete hain — professional nahi lagta, seriously out of place lagta hai. Sirf unka naam lo.

[Second mistake — saying "sir" and "madam."
Sweden has a first name culture — call even the boss by first name. If you say "yes sir" — it doesn't seem professional, seriously it seems out of place. Just use their name.]

Teesri galti — interview mein ikhtilaf se bachna.
Bahut baar Swedish interviewers aisi baat kehte hain jo slightly galat ho — ya aise assumption ke saath sawal poochte hain jo challenge kiya ja sakta hai. Woh dekh rahe hote hain ke kya aap politely disagree kar sakte hain ya sirf haan mein haan milaoge.

[Third mistake — avoiding disagreement in the interview.
Many times Swedish interviewers say something slightly wrong — or ask a question with an assumption that can be challenged. They're watching whether you can politely disagree or will just agree with everything.]

"Actually, from my experience, I'd look at this slightly differently..." — yeh Sweden mein strong signal hai. Pakistan mein yeh seedha ikhtilaf lagti hai.

["Actually, from my experience, I'd look at this slightly differently..." — this is a strong signal in Sweden. In Pakistan this feels like direct disagreement.]

Chauthi galti — zyada formal dress.
Yeh counterintuitive hai — lekin actually Sweden mein formal suit agar company casual hai toh over-dressed lagta hai. Pehle company ki LinkedIn aur website check karo. Stockholm ke zyada tar tech companies mein: clean dark jeans aur collared shirt — perfect.

[Fourth mistake — too formal dress.
This is counterintuitive — but actually in Sweden a formal suit if the company is casual looks over-dressed. First check the company's LinkedIn and website. In most Stockholm tech companies: clean dark jeans and collared shirt — perfect.]

Paanchvi galti — "I will do anything for this job."
Pakistan mein yeh lagan zahir karna hai. Sweden mein yeh bechari zahir karna hai. Swedish employers woh candidates prefer karte hain jo apni limits jaante hain aur clearly batate hain ke unhe kya chahiye.

[Fifth mistake — "I will do anything for this job."
In Pakistan this shows dedication. In Sweden this shows desperation. Swedish employers prefer candidates who know their limits and clearly say what they want.]

**(5:30 – 8:00) — EXACT PHRASES JO KAAM KARTE HAIN**

Sawal: "Tell me about yourself."

Pakistani jawab: "Main bohot mehnatii hoon. Main ek team player hoon. Main hamesha seekhne ke liye tayar hoon." — Yeh generic hai. 30 seconds mein dismiss ho jaata hai.

[Pakistani answer: "I'm very hardworking. I'm a team player. I'm always ready to learn." — This is generic. Gets dismissed in 30 seconds.]

Swedish jawab ka format:
"I'm a [role] with [X] years of experience in [specific area]. Most recently I [specific achievement with number]. I'm interested in this role because [specific reason about their company]. Outside of work, [one personal thing]."

Maslan: "I'm a backend developer with 4 years of experience in Python. Most recently I reduced our API response time by 40% by redesigning our caching layer. I'm interested in this role because I read about your work on climate data APIs and that genuinely excites me. Outside of work I coach kids' cricket on weekends."

---

Sawal: "Tell me about a time something failed."

Pakistani approach: galti minimize karo, vague jawab do, bahiri wajuhat batao.

Swedish jawab ka format: "The [project name] failed because I underestimated [specific thing]. My specific decision was [X]. The result was [Y]. What I permanently changed afterward was [Z]."

Specific failure + personal zimmedari + concrete sabaq = strong Swedish jawab.

---

Sawal: "Do you have any questions for us?"

Pakistani jawab: "No, I think you explained everything very well, thank you." — Sweden mein yeh low interest signal hai.

Swedish approach: 2 se 3 sawal poochho. Behtareen sawal:
"What does success look like in the first 90 days in this role?"
"What's the biggest challenge the team is currently facing?"
"How does the team handle disagreement or different technical opinions?"

Teesra sawal khaas taur par acha hai — yeh dikhata hai ke aap Swedish collaborative culture samjhte hain.

[Third question is especially good — it shows you understand Swedish collaborative culture.]

---

Salary ke baray mein: seedha baat karo. Pakistani candidates salary discuss karte hue uncomfortable feel karte hain. Sweden mein yeh basically normal hai. Agar poochha jaaye — ek range do, confidently. Research karo pehle: Glassdoor Sweden, LinkedIn Salary. Phir: "Based on my research for this role level in Stockholm, I'm looking at SEK [X] to [Y]. Does that align with your budget?" — yeh professional hai, bechari nahi.

[About salary: talk directly. Pakistani candidates feel uncomfortable discussing salary. In Sweden this is basically normal. If asked — give a range, confidently. Research first: Glassdoor Sweden, LinkedIn Salary. Then: "Based on my research for this role level in Stockholm, I'm looking at SEK [X] to [Y]. Does that align with your budget?" — this is professional, not desperation.]

---

**(8:00 – 9:30) — AGAR SWEDISH MEIN INTERVIEW HO**

Kuch companies — khaas taur par government, municipality, healthcare — Swedish mein interview karte hain.

[Some companies — especially government, municipality, healthcare — conduct interviews in Swedish.]

Agar aapka Swedish B1 level hai — yeh possible hai.

[If your Swedish is B1 level — this is possible.]

Pehle bolo: "Jag ber om ursäkt om mitt svenska inte är perfekt — jag lär mig fortfarande." Matlab: "Maafi chahta hoon agar meri Swedish perfect nahi — main abhi bhi seekh raha hoon." Swedish employers language level ke baray mein honesty ko appreciate karte hain.

[First say: "Jag ber om ursäkt om mitt svenska inte är perfekt — jag lär mig fortfarande." Meaning: "I apologize if my Swedish isn't perfect — I'm still learning." Swedish employers appreciate honesty about language level.]

Agar koi word yaad nahi aata — English mein switch karo, phir Swedish mein wapas aao. Rukna mat.

[If you don't remember a word — switch to English, then come back to Swedish. Don't freeze.]

Ek Pakistani nurse ne mujhe bataya: "Pehle interview mein main ne Swedish mein jawab diya, English mein switch kiya, phir Swedish mein wapas aayi. Unhone job di. Unhone kaha unhe Swedish nahi, attitude chahiye tha."

[A Pakistani nurse told me: "In my first interview I answered in Swedish, switched to English, came back to Swedish. They gave me the job. They said they didn't need Swedish, they needed attitude."]

**[RETENTION HOOK — ~70% mark]**

Ab woh baat bataata hoon jo bahut kam log discuss karte hain — interview ke baad kya karna chahiye. Sweden mein post-interview follow-up ek alag art hai. Galat follow-up se aap reject ho saktay hain chahe interview achha bhi gaya ho.

[Now I'll tell you what very few people discuss — what to do after the interview. Post-interview follow-up in Sweden is a different art. Wrong follow-up can get you rejected even if the interview went well.]

---

**(9:30 – 11:00) — POST-INTERVIEW**

24 ghante ke andar: thank you email. Short. Genuinely — gushing nahi. Format:

"Hi [Name], thank you for the conversation today. I particularly enjoyed discussing [specific topic]. I'm genuinely excited about [specific thing]. Looking forward to next steps. [Your name]"

3 se 4 sentences. Bas. "Main aapka yeh zindagi badlane wala mauqa milne se intehai shukriyaguzar hoon" — yeh seriously zyada hai.

[3 to 4 sentences. That's it. "I am extremely grateful for this life-changing opportunity" — that's seriously too much.]

---

Agar 5 business days mein response nahi aaya: ek follow-up. Ek line: "Hi [Name], just checking in — wanted to confirm you've received everything you need from my side."

Do follow-ups Sweden mein: bechari. Ek: professional.

[Two follow-ups in Sweden: desperation. One: professional.]

---

Agar reject ho gaye: feedback maangna bilkul acceptable hai. Email: "Hi [Name], thank you for letting me know. If you're open to sharing — is there specific feedback on my candidacy that would help me improve?"

Bahut si Swedish companies actually feedback deti hain. Yeh globally unusual hai — isse use karo.

[Many Swedish companies actually give feedback. This is unusual globally — use it.]

---

Aur akhir mein — LinkedIn connect karo interview ke baad, process ke doran nahi. Reject hone ke baad bhi connect karo. Swedish professional network chota hai. Aaj ka interviewer kal ka referral ho sakta hai.

[And finally — LinkedIn connect after the interview, not during the process. Connect even after rejection. Swedish professional network is small. Today's interviewer can be tomorrow's referral.]

[Summary: in a Swedish interview — take responsibility for your achievements, clearly say "I", politely disagree, share specific mistakes you yourself fixed, and follow up once.]
