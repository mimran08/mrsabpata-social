## SCRIPT

**(0:00 – 0:30) — HOOK**

Pehla Ramadan Sweden mein — main ne socha tha roohani lihaz se badal dene wala hoga.

[My first Ramadan in Sweden — I thought it would be spiritually transforming.]

Aur tha — lekin un wajuhat ki wajah se nahi jo main ne expect ki theen.

[And it was — but not for the reasons I expected.]

Main ne Sweden mein Ramadan guzara hai jab roza 19 ghante ka tha. Aur Ramadan guzara hai jab din sirf 9 ghante ka tha. Dono ne mujhe kuch diya jo mujhe pehle se maloom nahi tha.

[I've spent Ramadan in Sweden when the fast was 19 hours. And spent Ramadan when the day was only 9 hours. Both gave me something I didn't know before.]

Aaj yeh share karna chahta hoon — isliye nahi ke meri kahani khaas hai — balke isliye ke agar aap Sweden mein pehli baar Ramadan guzarne wale hain — aap tayar rahein.

[Today I want to share this — not because my story is special — but because if you're going to spend Ramadan in Sweden for the first time — you should be prepared.]

---

**(0:30 – 1:30) — CONTEXT**

Sweden mein roze ka waqt honestly mausam ke hisaab se bahut badalta hai.

[In Sweden fasting time honestly changes quite a bit by season.]

Ramadan agar June mein pare — roza 20 ghante tak ka ho sakta hai. Sehri 2:30 baje. Iftar raat 10:30 baje.

[If Ramadan falls in June — the fast can be up to 20 hours. Sehri at 2:30am. Iftar at 10:30 at night.]

Ramadan agar December ya January mein pare — roza 7 se 8 ghante ka. Fajr 6:30 baje. Maghrib dopahar 3 baje.

[If Ramadan falls in December or January — the fast is 7 to 8 hours. Fajr at 6:30am. Maghrib at 3 in the afternoon.]

Ek hi iman — bilkul mukhtalif tajurba — sirf geography ki wajah se.

[The same faith — completely different experience — just because of geography.]

Aur yeh geographical dimension Pakistan mein rehte hue sochna honestly mushkil tha.

[And this geographical dimension was honestly hard to think about while living in Pakistan.]

---

**(1:30 – 4:00) — SUMMER RAMADAN — JAB ROZA 19 GHANTE KA HOTA HAI**

Main aapko ek specific din describe karta hoon.

[Let me describe one specific day to you.]

Raat 1:45 baj rahay the. Main kitchen mein tha, sehri bana raha tha. Bahar — June ka Sweden — aadha andheera tha. Woh specific roshni jo Sweden mein garmiyon mein hoti hai jab raat kabhi poori nahi aati — ek tarah ki permanent godhali.

[It was 1:45 at night. I was in the kitchen making sehri. Outside — Sweden's June — half dark. That specific light that exists in Sweden in summer when night never fully arrives — a kind of permanent twilight.]

Sehri ka waqt 2:17 baje tha. Main ne khaya. Namaz padhi.

[Sehri time was 2:17am. I ate. Prayed.]

Office 8 baje pohonchna tha. Meetings, code reviews, presentations. Lunch par sab kuch order kar rahe the — main ne paani liya. Ek Swedish colleague ne poocha: "Kuch nahi khaoge?"

[Had to reach office at 8am. Meetings, code reviews, presentations. At lunch everyone was ordering things — I took water. A Swedish colleague asked: "You won't eat anything?"]

Main ne bataya — Ramadan. Woh genuinely curious tha. "Kab tak?" "Abhi agar bahar dekho — jab tak woh raat wali dhandlaahat nahi aa jaati."

[I explained — Ramadan. He was genuinely curious. "Until when?" "Right now if you look outside — until when that evening dimness doesn't come."]

Woh khidki ke paas gaya. Hum dono ne bahar dekha. Raat ke 10 baj rahe the — bahar abhi bhi roshan tha. "That's... that's really long," usne kaha. Main ne kaha haan.

[He went to the window. We both looked outside. It was 10 at night — outside was still bright. "That's... that's really long," he said. I said yes.]

Woh moment. Jab kisi ne — bina poori tarah samjhe — actually socha. Woh moment mujhe aaj bhi yaad hai.

[That moment. When someone — without fully understanding — actually thought. I still remember that moment today.]

---

Sweden mein Muslim scholars aur Islamiska Förbundet ka ek qabol shuda fatwa hai: jin countries mein din ka waqt intehai zyada ya kam ho, wahan ke Muslims ek qareeb mulk ka waqt follow kar saktay hain — jaise Norway ya Denmark — ya Makkah ka waqt follow kar saktay hain. 19 ghante ka roza agar health ya capacity allow nahi karti toh lazmi nahi hai. Yeh Islamiska Förbundet ne publicly state kiya hai — aap directly unki website par check kar saktay hain.

[In Sweden Muslim scholars and Islamiska Förbundet have a widely accepted fatwa: in countries where day time is extremely long or short, Muslims there can follow a neighbouring country's time — like Norway or Denmark — or can follow Makkah's time. A 19-hour fast is not compulsory if health or capacity doesn't allow it. This has been publicly stated by Islamiska Förbundet — you can check directly on their website.]

---

**(4:00 – 6:00) — WINTER RAMADAN — JAB ROZA 8 GHANTE KA HOTA HAI**

Winter Ramadan — bilkul mukhtalif. Maghrib dopahar 3 baje.

[Winter Ramadan — completely different. Maghrib at 3 in the afternoon.]

Pehli baar jab aisa hua — main office mein tha. 3 baje ka alarm. Bina kisi ko disturb kiye — bathroom mein gaya, quietly iftar kiya — khajoor aur paani — aur 5 minute mein wapas.

[When this happened for the first time — I was in office. 3pm alarm. Without disturbing anyone — went to bathroom, quietly did iftar — dates and water — and back in 5 minutes.]

Yeh tajurba roohani lihaz se khubsoorat bhi tha aur ajeeb bhi. Pakistan mein iftar ek ijtamai lamha hota hai — khandan, khana, dua sab saath. Sweden mein — akela bathroom mein, office building mein, khajoor haath mein.

[This experience was both spiritually beautiful and strange. In Pakistan iftar is a collective moment — family, food, prayer all together. In Sweden — alone in bathroom, in an office building, dates in hand.]

Lekin us moment mein kuch aur bhi tha.

[But there was something else in that moment too.]

Jab sab kuch strip ho jaata hai — khandan, riwayat, ijtamai rasam — jo bachta hai woh sirf aap aur Allah ke darmiyan hota hai. Woh khaalis lamha. Koi audience nahi. Koi tawaqo nahi. Sirf niyat.

[When everything gets stripped away — family, tradition, collective ritual — what remains is only between you and Allah. That pure moment. No audience. No expectation. Only intention.]

Main ne Sweden mein Ramadan mein arguably zyada khaalis roohani tajurba feel kiya Pakistan ke bahut saale Ramadanon se zyada.

[I arguably felt a more pure spiritual experience in Sweden's Ramadan than in many years of Ramadan in Pakistan.]

---

**[RETENTION HOOK — ~40% mark]**

Ab woh practical cheezein bataata hoon jo Sweden mein Ramadan ko actually manageable banati hain. Aur woh Swedish workplace situations jo saamne aayenge — aur unhe exactly kaise handle karein.

[Now I'll tell you those practical things that actually make Ramadan manageable in Sweden. And those Swedish workplace situations that will come up — and how to handle them exactly.]

---

**(6:00 – 8:30) — SWEDISH WORKPLACE MEIN RAMADAN**

Sweden mein mazhabi reayat qanoonan protected hai. Swedish Discrimination Act mein faith-based accommodations shamil hain.

[In Sweden religious accommodation is legally protected. Faith-based accommodations are included in the Swedish Discrimination Act.]

Namaz:
Seedha, professionally bolo: "I observe five daily prayers as part of my faith. They take about 5 to 8 minutes each. Would it be possible to use part of my break time for this?"

Zyada tar Swedish employers: haan. Koi masla nahi.

[Most Swedish employers: yes. No problem.]

Ramadan ki ittala:
Pehle din inform karo. Informally. Email nahi — bas colleagues ko batao. "By the way, this month I'm fasting — won't be at lunch, might have some timing adjustments."

Swedish colleagues genuinely curious honge. Yeh curiosity judging nahi — yeh dilchaspi hai.

[Swedish colleagues will be genuinely curious. This curiosity isn't judging — it's interest.]

Office mein iftar:
Agar iftar office hours mein pare — break room mein quietly khajoor aur paani — bilkul theek hai. Koi nahi rokay ga. Agar comfortable ho — colleagues ko batao. Zyada tar ko interesting lagta hai, disturbing nahi.

[If iftar falls during office hours — quietly dates and water in break room — completely fine. Nobody will stop you. If comfortable — tell colleagues. Most find it interesting, not disturbing.]

Summer Ramadan mein late start:
Agar sehri raat 2 baje ke qareeb ho — employer se baat kar saktay ho: "This month I'm eating at 2am for religious reasons — would it be possible to start at 9am instead of 8am?"

Swedish flexible work culture mein yeh ek reasonable request hai.

[In Swedish flexible work culture this is a reasonable request.]

---

Swedes Ramadan ke baray mein genuinely poochte hain — aur kabhi kabhi aise sawal aate hain jin ki tawaqo nahi hoti.

[Swedes genuinely ask about Ramadan — and sometimes questions come that aren't expected.]

"Paani bhi nahi?" — nahi.
"Kaise nahi mar jaate?" — muskrao aur kaho: "The body adapts. It's actually remarkable what it can do."
"Yeh volunteer hai?" — haan. "Kyun?" — yeh ek acha mauqa hai samjhaane ka.

["Not even water?" — no.
"How do you not die?" — smile and say: "The body adapts. It's actually remarkable what it can do."
"Is this voluntary?" — yes. "Why?" — this is a good opportunity to explain.]

Yeh conversations — Sweden mein — aapko apne iman ke baray mein zyada soch samajh kar sochne par majboor karti hain. Yeh ek unexpected tohfa hai.

[These conversations — in Sweden — force you to think more thoughtfully about your faith. This is an unexpected gift.]

---

**(8:30 – 10:30) — COMMUNITY — SWEDEN MEIN RAMADAN KA IJTAMAI DIMENSION**

Sweden mein 80,000 se zyada Muslims hain. Stockholm mein 40,000 ke qareeb. Masajid, Islamic centers, Pakistani organizations — sab maujood hain.

[In Sweden there are more than 80,000 Muslims. About 40,000 in Stockholm. Mosques, Islamic centers, Pakistani organizations — all are present.]

Stockholm ke main centers:
Islamiska Förbundet i Sverige — Sweden ki sabse bari Muslim umbrella organization.
Stockholms Moské — Södermalm mein — Juma par bohot active.
NordPak Foundation — regular Ramadan iftars karta hai.
Botkyrka mein Swedish-Pakistani masjid — Pakistan se aane walon ko ghar jaisa ahsaas deta hai.

[Stockholm's main centers:
Islamiska Förbundet i Sverige — Sweden's largest Muslim umbrella organization.
Stockholms Moské — in Södermalm — very active for Friday prayers.
NordPak Foundation — organizes regular Ramadan iftars.
In Botkyrka there's a Swedish-Pakistani mosque — gives a home-like feeling to those coming from Pakistan.]

Sweden mein ek riwayat barh rahi hai — "Open Iftar" — jahan masajid aur Islamic centers ghayr muslimaan ko bhi iftar par invite karte hain. Agar aapke Swedish colleagues curious hain — unhe ek community iftar par invite karo. Dono taraf se seriously amazing experience hota hai.

[In Sweden a tradition is growing — "Open Iftar" — where mosques and Islamic centers also invite non-Muslims to iftar. If your Swedish colleagues are curious — invite them to a community iftar. It's a seriously amazing experience from both sides.]

---

**[RETENTION HOOK — ~70% mark]**

Ab woh baat share karna chahta hoon jo mujhe Sweden mein Ramadan mein actually mili — jo Pakistan mein kabhi nahi mili thi. Yeh woh hai jis ke liye main aaj bhi Sweden ka shukriya ada karta hoon.

[Now I want to share what I actually found in Ramadan in Sweden — that I'd never found in Pakistan. This is what I'm still grateful to Sweden for today.]

**(10:00 – 12:00) — JO SWEDEN NE RAMADAN KE BARAY MEIN SIKHAYA**

Pakistan mein — yeh bade adab se keh raha hoon, koi tanqeed nahi — Ramadan ek ijtamai performance bhi hai. Sab roza rakhte hain. Sab iftar ke waqt khaate hain. Sab tarawih mein jaate hain. Sab ek saath hain.

[In Pakistan — I'm saying this with great respect, no criticism — Ramadan is also a collective performance. Everyone fasts. Everyone eats at iftar time. Everyone goes for Tarawih. Everyone is together.]

Sweden mein — koi nahi dekh raha. Koi social pressure nahi. Koi maahol nahi jo aapko majboor kare.

[In Sweden — nobody is watching. No social pressure. No environment forcing you.]

Sirf aap hain aur woh ehad jo aap ne khud se kiya hai.

[Just you and the commitment you made to yourself.]

Aur main ne pehli baar Sweden mein realize kiya ke mera Ramadan actually MERA tha. Community ka nahi, khandan ka nahi, muasharay ka nahi — mera.

[And for the first time in Sweden I realized that my Ramadan was actually MINE. Not the community's, not the family's, not society's — mine.]

---

"Jab koi nahi dekh raha hota — tab aapko pata chalta hai ke aap kar kyun rahe hain. Aur woh jaanna — woh wuzaahat — Pakistan mein hujaum mein dhundna mushkil thi. Sweden ki khamoshi mein woh saaf ho gayi."

["When nobody is watching — then you understand why you're doing it. And that knowing — that clarity — was hard to find in the crowd in Pakistan. In Sweden's silence it became clear."]

---

Yeh meri personal discovery hai. Aap ki alag hogi. Lekin main chahta hoon ke aap sirf is liye mayoos na hon kyunke Sweden ka Ramadan Pakistan wala Ramadan nahi hoga.

[This is my personal discovery. Yours will be different. But I want you not to be disappointed just because Sweden's Ramadan won't be Pakistan's Ramadan.]

Sweden wala Ramadan kuch aur deta hai.

[Sweden's Ramadan gives something else.]
