[Sweden's citizenship rules have changed — and seriously, those who are in Sweden or planning to come, absolutely need to hear this.]

29 April 2026 ko Sweden ki Parliament ne ek law pass kiya. Aur us law ki wajah se — jo path pehle 5 saal mein complete hota tha, woh ab 8 saal ka ho gaya hai. Teen saal ka farq. Teen saal.

[On April 29th 2026 Sweden's Parliament passed a law. And because of that law — the path that used to complete in 5 years has now become 8 years. Three years' difference. Three years.]

Aur yeh sirf ek number ka change nahi hai. Aaj main aapko poori baat bataata hoon.

[And this isn't just a change in a number. Today I'll tell you the full story.]

**(0:30 – 1:30) — CONTEXT**

Basically seedha baat karta hoon.

[Basically straight talk.]

Pehle Sweden mein citizenship ke liye 5 saal rehna padta tha. That's it. 5 saal lawfully Sweden mein raho, application do, Swedish citizenship mil jaati thi — relatively easily.

[Before, you needed to live in Sweden for 5 years to get citizenship. That's it. Live lawfully in Sweden for 5 years, apply, Swedish citizenship would come — relatively easily.]

Ab woh 5 se directly 8 ho gaye hain. Aur saath mein — Swedish language ka test bhi dena hoga. Civics ka test bhi. Aur financially self-sufficient bhi prove karna hoga.

[Now that's gone directly from 5 to 8. And alongside — you'll also need to give a Swedish language test. Civics test too. And prove financial self-sufficiency as well.]

Yeh sab 6 June 2026 se effective ho raha hai.

[All of this becomes effective from June 6th 2026.]

**(1:30 – 4:00) — TEEN SAAL KA ASLI MATLAB**

Okay so, yeh teen saal ka difference practically kya hota hai? Main aapko real terms mein bataata hoon.

[Okay so, what does this three-year difference practically mean? Let me tell you in real terms.]

Maano aap 2023 mein Sweden aaye. Old rules ke hisaab se, 2028 mein citizenship apply kar saktay thay. Passport milta. EU mein freely travel karte. Jobs mein jahan "Swedish citizenship preferred" likha hota hai, wahan bhi apply kar saktay.

[Say you came to Sweden in 2023. Under the old rules, you could apply for citizenship in 2028. Passport would come. Travel freely in EU. Could apply to jobs where it says "Swedish citizenship preferred" too.]

Ab? 2031. Teen saal aur.

[Now? 2031. Three more years.]

Aur yeh teen saal sirf rehne waale nahi hain — in teen salon mein language test pass karna hai, civics test pass karna hai, aur income ka jawab bhi dena hai.

[And these aren't just years of staying — in these three years you have to pass a language test, civics test, and account for your income too.]

Main aapko ek shaks ke baray mein bataata hoon — 4 saal pehle Sweden aaya. Pakistani. Engineering background. Sochta raha "pehle settle hota hoon, phir dekhunga." Swedish nahi seekhi. Yeh socha ke baad mein kar lega.

[Let me tell you about one person — came to Sweden 4 years ago. Pakistani. Engineering background. He kept thinking "let me settle first, then I'll see." Didn't learn Swedish. Thought he'd do it later.]

Jab main ne usse mila — woh keh raha tha "yaar, honestly mujhe pata hi nahi tha ke yeh itna important hoga." Ab woh June ke baad 8 saal ki count mein hoga. Aur ab Swedish seekhne ki koshish kar raha hai — 4 saal baad. Jo usne 4 saal pehle shuru kiya hota, aaj woh B2 level par hota.

[When I met him — he was saying "yaar, honestly I didn't know this would be this important." Now he'll be in the 8-year count after June. And now he's trying to learn Swedish — 4 years later. What he had started 4 years ago, today he'd be at B2 level.]

Uski galti yeh nahi thi ke usne Swedish nahi seekhi. Uski galti yeh thi ke usne socha tha ke time hai.

[His mistake wasn't that he didn't learn Swedish. His mistake was that he thought there was time.]

**(4:00 – 6:00) — LANGUAGE TEST**

Ab language test ke baray mein baat karte hain.

[Now let's talk about the language test.]

Abhi tak Sweden ne officially declare nahi kiya ke exactly kaunsa level required hoga. Lekin main ne neighbouring countries ke patterns dekhe hain, government documents padhe hain — aur mera personal prediction hai ke B1 se neeche nahi hoga.

[Sweden hasn't officially declared yet exactly what level will be required. But I've looked at neighbouring countries' patterns, read government documents — and my personal prediction is it won't be below B1.]

Denmark mein A2 se B1 chahiye. Norway mein B1. Finland mein A2. Sweden ki current government ka stance dekh ke — yeh B1 par settle hogi ya us se upar. Neeche nahi.

[Denmark needs A2 to B1. Norway needs B1. Finland A2. Looking at Sweden's current government's stance — it'll settle at B1 or above. Not below.]

B1 practically kya hota hai? Matlab aap roz ki life ki conversations samajh sako. Office mein basic communication ho sake. Swedish doctor ko apni problem explain kar sako. News ka gist samajh aa jaye.

[What does B1 practically mean? That you can understand everyday life conversations. Basic communication in office can happen. Can explain your problem to a Swedish doctor. Can understand the gist of the news.]

Yeh koi rocket science nahi hai. Lekin yaar, agar aap Sweden mein hain aur SFI nahi kar rahe — woh sabse badi mistake hai jo aap kar rahe hain. SFI completely free hai. Municipality provide karti hai. Aur ab yeh sirf ek optional activity nahi rahi — ab iska directly citizenship se connection hai.

[This isn't rocket science. But yaar, if you're in Sweden and not doing SFI — that's the biggest mistake you're making. SFI is completely free. Municipality provides it. And now this isn't just an optional activity — now it has a direct connection to citizenship.]

[The government isn't defining exactly how much and exactly how yet. What's in the draft is — you'll need to show you're not dependent on government welfare — socialbidrag — for a certain period.]

[Taking welfare in the first years of coming to Sweden — there's no shame in this. It's part of the system. The Swedish state built this so people can get on their feet. But if you're seriously thinking toward citizenship and are on welfare for a long time — then this can become a problem in the future.]

Jitna jaldi apne paon par khare ho jaayein — utna better.
